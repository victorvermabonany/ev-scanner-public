Info

Plain-English notes on EV, probability, arbitrage betting, and the tradeoffs that matter.

EV Betting, Clearly

EV means expected value. It answers one question: “If I could make this same bet many times, would I expect to make money?”

EV = true probability × decimal odds - 1

If your true probability is higher than the sportsbook’s implied probability, the bet is +EV.

How To Read An EV Line
  • Sportsbook: where the app says to find the bet.
  • Odds: the price you need. If it moves, EV changes.
  • True prob: the app’s fair win probability after removing vig.
  • Book implied: what the sportsbook price is saying.
  • Fair odds: the no-vig price. Better than fair is the edge.
EV Example

A book offers +100. That means the book implies about 50%.

If the fair no-vig probability is 55%, then EV is 55% × 2.00 - 1 = +10%.

That does not mean it will win today. It means the price is good long term.

EV Pros
  • Works on normal single bets.
  • Can scale across many sports and books.
  • Teaches you to care about price, not just picks.
  • CLV can show whether your process is improving.
EV Cons
  • You can lose lots of +EV bets in a row.
  • Bad reference lines create fake edges.
  • Small edges need volume and patience.
  • Overbetting can ruin a good strategy.
Arbitrage Betting, Clearly

Arbitrage means covering every outcome at prices that guarantee profit. You are not trying to predict the winner. You are using price differences between books.

Arb exists when all best-outcome implied probabilities add up to less than 100%
How To Read An Arb
  • Each leg must be the same event and same market.
  • Each leg must be placed at the sportsbook shown.
  • Stake amounts are sized so payout is equalized.
  • If one price changes, recalculate before betting.
Arb Pros
  • Profit is locked if every leg is placed correctly.
  • Does not require predicting who wins.
  • Great for finding books with stale prices.
Arb Cons
  • Lines can move before every leg is placed.
  • Books may limit, void, or grade markets differently.
  • Live arbs are fragile because prices move fast.
  • You need money available at multiple books.
Live Betting Rules
  • Verify odds at the book before acting.
  • Do not trust stale live prices.
  • Use smaller stakes when lines are moving fast.
  • If the exact line is gone, skip or rescan.
